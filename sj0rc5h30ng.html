The spatial statistics performed on the digital elevation model revealed that the average elevation of the buildings that may flood in 2015 is 7.9 feet, in 2020 it is 11.6 feet, in 2050 it is 10.8 feet and in 2080 14.4 feet. Those that never flood under any scenario (31 properties) have a diverse range of elevations, going from 12 feet to 60 feet. This means that other factors rather than elevation should also be taken into account, because the mean elevation calculated here takes into account the building parcel and not streets morphology. The table below compares the elevation results between Borough, H&A properties and all the buildings in Central and East Harlem. A contributing factor to Hope Properties flooding more than Ascendants' is topographical elevation. Hope Properties are at lower median elevation (13.2 feet) than Ascendants (29 feet) and also lower than the median elevation of both Central (22 feet) and East Harlem (15 feet).
